    <p class="section-kicker">Collection History</p>
    <h2 class="record-section-title">How the collection took shape</h2>
    <p>The Jackson State University Art Collection reflects decades of collecting in support of cultural and civic engagement for students and the public. Its roots extend to Lawrence Arthur Jones, the first chair of the Art Department, who assembled works to widen students’ access to global artistic traditions and forms of cultural expression.</p>
    <p>Today the collection is housed in Johnson Hall, a 30,000-square-foot facility that includes climate-controlled storage for the university’s art holdings. That physical care infrastructure matters, but so does intellectual access: the collection serves as a teaching resource, a research asset, and a foundation for future curatorial work and collections care.</p>
    <p>This digital project forms part of that effort. The site is being revised to move beyond brief object labels and toward fuller records that emphasize culture and community, place, use, material, subject, and provenance wherever the available evidence permits. The aim is not only to make the collection easier to search, but also to interpret it more responsibly.</p>
