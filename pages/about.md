    <p class="section-kicker">Collection History</p>
    <h2 class="record-section-title">How the collection took shape</h2>
    <p>The Jackson State University Art Collection reflects decades of collecting in support of cultural and civic engagement for students and the public. Its roots go back to Lawrence Arthur Jones, the first chair of the Art Department, who assembled works to widen students’ access to global artistic traditions and forms of cultural expression.</p>
    <p>Today the collection is housed in Johnson Hall, a 30,000 square foot facility that includes climate-controlled storage for the university’s art holdings. That physical care infrastructure matters, but so does intellectual access: the collection functions as a teaching resource, a research asset, and a basis for future curatorial and collections-management work.</p>
    <p>This digital project is part of that work. The site is being revised to move beyond thin object labels and toward fuller records that emphasize culture and community, place, use, material, subject, and provenance wherever the available evidence allows. The goal is not only to make the collection easier to search, but to make it more responsible to interpret.</p>
  </article>
